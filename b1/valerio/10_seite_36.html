Grammar Rules and Observations

1. Präteritum (Simple Past Tense)

This story is primarily told in the simple past tense (Präteritum), which is very common for written narratives and formal storytelling in German. You can see many examples of both "strong" (irregular) and "weak" (regular) verbs.

Weak Verbs (add -te):

"Ich dachte, Luis wäre wach geworden..." (I thought...)

"Aus dem Bad hörte ich ein leises, metallisches 'Weitermachen!'" (From the bathroom I heard...)

"Entschlossen öffnete ich die Tür..." (Determined, I opened the door...)

Strong Verbs (vowel change):

"...aber er schlief." (...but he slept.)

"Dabei kam ich am Bad vorbei." (In the process, I came past the bathroom.)

"Die Zahnbürste... lag auf dem Fußboden..." (The toothbrush... lay on the floor...)

2. Subordinate Clauses (Nebensätze)

Subordinate clauses are dependent clauses that add extra information to the main clause. In German, they are famous for sending the conjugated verb to the very end of the clause. This text uses several types.

Causal with 'weil' (because): The verb 'hörte' is at the end.

"Eines Nachts wachte ich auf, weil ich eine leise Stimme hörte."

Object clause with 'dass' (that): The helping verb 'hat' is at the end.

"Ob es nicht reicht, dass er sich gestern die Zähne besonders sorgfältig geputzt hat."

Relative Clause with 'die' (which/that): The verb 'war' is at the end.

"Sie befand sich hinter einer Klappe, die mit einer winzigen Schraube verschlossen war."

3. Reflexive Verbs

Reflexive verbs are actions where the subject and the object are the same person/thing. They require a reflexive pronoun (mich, dich, sich, uns, euch, sich). The pronoun can be in the accusative or dative case.

Accusative (the action is done to oneself directly):

"...und machte mich auf den Weg..." (...and I made my way...)

"...und schnitt mich." (...and cut myself.)

Dative (often when a body part is involved, the body part becomes the direct object):

"Wenn Luis sich damit die Zähne zu putzen begann..." (When Luis began to brush his [own] teeth with it...)
